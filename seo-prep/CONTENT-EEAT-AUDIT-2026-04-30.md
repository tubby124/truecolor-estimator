## Scoring Methodology

Each page scored 1–10 across 7 dimensions for a total of /70.

1. **Experience** — first-person/in-house signals (Roland UV, in-house designer, local pickup)
2. **Expertise** — material specs, turnaround precision, technical accuracy
3. **Authoritativeness** — local NAP, brand mentions, address/phone
4. **Trust** — real prices (no "contact for pricing"), real phone, real location
5. **AI citation readiness** — quotable atomic facts, structured price data, scannable FAQ
6. **Brand voice compliance** — forbidden phrases absent, real numbers used
7. **Thin-content risk inverse** — word count >400, FAQ ≥8, internal links ≥2 → 10/10

**Note:** Word counts below are estimated from description + descriptionNode prose only (DDG captions and FAQ text excluded from "body word count" but inform AI citation score).

## Cross-Page Patterns Worth Locking

These observations apply across all 7 pages and should inform any future ranking-page builds:

1. **Local NAP repetition correlates with score.** Every 10/10 Authoritativeness page has 216 33rd St W AND (306) 954-8688 in the body prose, not just metadata. Flyer + aluminum pages lose points specifically here.
2. **DDG presence correlates with content score.** Both sub-60 pages (aluminum, sticker) lack DesignDirectionGrid. The DDG component does double duty: visual SERP signal + ~80 words of caption text + image-sitemap fodder.
3. **8 FAQs is the working floor.** All sub-60 pages have 6. All 60+ pages have 8. AI citation readiness scales linearly with FAQ count up to ~10.
4. **Tier-priced FAQs out-cite single-price FAQs.** "$45 / $65 / $110" tier breakdowns get cited; "starting at $45" alone gets summarized away.
5. **Forbidden-phrase compliance is excellent across the board.** Single voice flag = sign-company "fast / quality" line. Brand-voice rule is being followed by hand authoring.
6. **Competitor naming is a unique differentiator.** Only `business-cards-saskatoon` names Staples + FedEx Office directly with prices. This is exceptional AI citation bait and should be replicated where defensible (e.g., "VistaPrint" mention on flyer page if pricing comparison is honest).
